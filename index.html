Problem

Steam enters a turbine at 3 MPa and 400°C with an enthalpy of 3230.9 kJ/kg and exits at 0.1 MPa with an enthalpy of 2675.5 kJ/kg. The mass flow rate is 5 kg/s. Assume steady-state, adiabatic operation. Find the power output of the turbine.

Step-by-step solution

Given

Inlet: P1 = 3 MPa, T1 = 400°C, h1 = 3230.9 kJ/kg
Exit: P2 = 0.1 MPa, h2 = 2675.5 kJ/kg
Mass flow rate: ˙m = 5 kg/s  |  Adiabatic, steady-state

Governing equation

˙Wout = ˙m · (h1 − h2)

Substituting

Δh = 3230.9 − 2675.5 = 555.4 kJ/kg
˙Wout = 5 kg/s × 555.4 kJ/kg = 2777 kJ/s

Power output = 2777 kW = 2.777 MW
Enthalpy values from thermodynamic tables are approximated. Verify h1 and h2 against your textbook steam tables before submitting work.
Problem

A Carnot heat engine operates between a high-temperature reservoir at 800 K and a low-temperature reservoir at 300 K. The engine absorbs 500 kJ of heat from the hot reservoir per cycle. Find (a) the thermal efficiency and (b) the net work output per cycle.

Step-by-step solution

Given

TH = 800 K  |  TL = 300 K  |  QH = 500 kJ

Part (a): Thermal efficiency

ηCarnot = 1 − TL / TH

η = 1 − (300 / 800) = 1 − 0.375 = 0.625

Thermal efficiency = 62.5%

Part (b): Net work output

Wnet = η × QH

Wnet = 0.625 × 500 kJ = 312.5 kJ

Net work output = 312.5 kJ per cycle
